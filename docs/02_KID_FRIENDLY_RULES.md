# 02 — Kid-Friendly Rules

Squishy Smash targets a storybook audience of roughly ages 4–8, while still being fun for a broader Roblox audience.

## Tone

Wholesome, colorful, gentle, funny, soft, safe, parent-friendly, collectible, magical, and cozy.

## Hard no list

Do not include violence, weapons, combat, killing, damage language in player-facing UI, blood/gore, horror/jump scares, vulgarity, sexual content, dating/romance mechanics, gambling-style wording, predatory monetization, or scary punishment systems.

## Allowed action words

Use squish, squeeze, bounce, boop, pop, wobble, giggle, sparkle, collect, discover, help, rescue, decorate, unlock, restore, cheer up, and wake up.

## Avoid player-facing words

Avoid attack, damage, kill, destroy, defeat, enemy, weapon, battle, hit points, boss fight, loot box, gamble, horror, and scary.

## Terminology replacements

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Health | Joy Meter |
| Damage | Squish Points |
| Attack Power | Squish Power |
| Enemy | Squishy Friend |
| Defeated | Happy Pop |
| Battle Zone / Arena | Play Zone |
| Loot | Rewards / Stickers / Sparkles |
| Pull | Discover |
| Burst Type | Pop Style |
| Impact Sounds | Squish Sounds |
| Burst Sound | Happy Pop Sound |

Players should never feel like they are hurting a living character. Interaction should feel like giving a sleepy squishy friend a silly squish, filling its Joy Meter, and watching it Happy Pop into sparkles and rewards.

Moonlit Hollow can be soft spooky, but never scary. Ghosts, bats, moonlight, and starry eyes should feel cute, plush, and gentle.
