# 01 — Squishy Smash Universe Canon

## Core story idea

Squishy Smash is a soft, playful world where feelings become places and squishy friends help each other feel seen.

The storybook manuscript introduces three first-feeling worlds:

| Storybook World | First Feeling | Roblox Zone | Signature Sound | Main Guide |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pudding Hills | Comfort | Pudding Hills | Pmf | Soft Dumpling |
| Goo Coast | Surprise | Goo Coast | Sploink | Goo Ball |
| Moonlit Hollow | Brave-Cuddle | Moonlit Hollow | Thup | Blushy Bun Bunny |

Above the worlds is **The Sparkle**: the light that comes from being found.

## Roblox premise

The Sparkle has scattered into little glimmers across the Squishy world. Players explore each land, help sleepy squishy friends, collect Sparkle Coins, discover character cards, and restore more light to the world.

The player is not fighting anyone. The player is helping, discovering, collecting, decorating, and making squishies happy.

## Three launch worlds

### Pudding Hills
Warm, cozy, creamy, food-squishy land. This is the starter zone. Characters come from `launch_squishy_foods.json`.

### Goo Coast
Glossy, surprising, bouncy land. Characters come from `goo_fidgets_drop_01.json`.

### Moonlit Hollow
Soft spooky, never scary. A gentle night-world about brave cuddles. Characters come from `creepy_cute_pack_01.json`.

## Mythic/Core characters

These are special storybook-level characters. Do not treat them as enemies.

- Celestial Dumpling Core
- Singularity Goo Core
- Mythic Plush Familiar

They are guardians / ancient friends / Sparkle-level figures. They should feel rare, beautiful, and protective.

## Signature chant

Use these as brand signatures: `Pmf`, `Sploink`, `Thup`, and `Everybody Squish!`.

Possible Roblox uses: zone intro sounds, card reveal sounds, group event chant, tutorial moments, quest completion, and Sparkle restoration events.

## Storybook and iOS connection

The iOS app is the simple touch/ASMR collection experience. The Roblox game is the explorable world. Both should share character names, card numbers, rarities, pack names, card art, sounds, kid-friendly tone, and Sparkle lore.
