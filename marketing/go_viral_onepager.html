Because time is the scarcest resource

One 2–3 hour Sunday batch → a week of daily posts

Never a daily scramble. Capture once, chop, voice, assemble from reusable templates, schedule the week.

  1. Capture · ~25 min
    One screen-grab pass in the live game for fresh B-roll.
  2. Chop
    CapCut clip bin: squish / pop / reveal / POV / page.
  3. Voice
    All week's scripts through ElevenLabs in one dad-voice session — owned, zero takedown risk.
  4. Assemble
    Fill the 8 templates; brand overlay + owned stinger as the end-button. Frame 1 is already mid-motion.
  5. Schedule
    Load the week into Metricool — after-school + evening + weekend mornings, tied to the week's code.
  6. Distribute owned
    Mirror to Discord, one Reddit devlog, the hub, the Group shout.
Cadence: launch sprint 1/day cross-posted (weeks 1–4) → sustained floor 5/week. Promise “one batch a week, posts land daily” — never “daily forever.” If a dad-week hits, the scheduled queue keeps the account alive.

Non-negotiable — the Law of marketing

The kid-safety guardrails

The safe version is more on-brand and more shareable. These aren't a tax — they're the wedge.

Characters, not faces
The girls appear only as their in-game cards + a first name. No faces, no real-child voices, no school or location. Co-creators, never the punchline.
Never collect data from kids
Email opt-in is parent-gated only. In-game CTAs only ever say “ask a grown-up.” Book & code funnels are data-free by design.
Kids stay on Roblox
Community for 6–9s lives in the Roblox Group + in-game. Discord is 13+ — parent/creator-only, never dangled at kids.
“No loot boxes” stays literally true
Capsules free forever; codes grant small coin gifts; style & convenience only. It's enforced in code — never let gacha-FOMO framing creep in.
Own your audio
Lead with owned SFX & music (takedown-proof); apply trend sounds only to interactive formats. Keep making-of content general-audience.
Ride the category, not a trademark
Own the original “Squishy Smash” world & its names. Never imply affiliation with Squishmallows; never name a competitor.
